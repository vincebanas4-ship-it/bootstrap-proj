Marine Tourism

Marine Tourism

Dive beneath the surface and discover the extraordinary underwater world of Zamboanga del Norte's seas and reefs.

Zamboanga del Norte is flanked by two of the Philippines' richest bodies of water — the Sulu Sea and the Bohol Sea — both of which support extraordinary marine biodiversity. The province's coastal waters are home to vibrant coral reef ecosystems, diverse fish species, sea turtles, and marine mammals, making it a destination of significant interest for divers, snorkelers, and marine life enthusiasts.

Marine protected areas (MPAs) established by coastal municipalities protect reef systems and replenish fish stocks, while dive operators in Dapitan City and Dipolog offer guided underwater experiences ranging from beginner snorkeling trips to advanced technical dives on submerged reefs and walls.

Marine & Diving Destinations

Explore the coral reefs, marine sanctuaries, and dive sites of Zamboanga del Norte.

Protected Reef

Aliguay Island Marine Sanctuary

One of the best dive and snorkel sites in the province, Aliguay Island's surrounding waters feature healthy hard and soft coral gardens, diverse reef fish, and excellent visibility. A marine protected area with guided snorkel and dive tours.

Dapitan City Marine Sanctuary
Dive Site

Sibutad Reef System

The clear waters surrounding Sibutad municipality are known for their extensive reef systems and outstanding visibility. Popular dive sites include coral walls, fish schools, and occasional sightings of sea turtles and reef sharks.

Sibutad Reef Diving
Sea Turtle Habitat

Sigayan Bay Seagrass & Turtle Zone

The seagrass beds and shallow reef flats of Sigayan Bay serve as critical habitat for green sea turtles and dugongs. Guided snorkel tours are available through the local barangay marine sanctuary committee.

Sibutad Marine Conservation
Resort Diving

Dakak Reef

The reef fronting Dakak Beach Resort offers accessible snorkeling directly from the beach, with colorful reef fish and coral gardens starting in shallow water — perfect for beginner snorkelers and first-time divers.

Dapitan City Resort Reef
Fishing Heritage

Dipolog City Fish Landing Port

The main fishing port of Dipolog City is a fascinating window into the province's maritime culture — early morning fish markets offer encounters with the daily catch and the fishing communities that harvest the province's rich seas.

Dipolog City Fishing Port
Estuary Ecosystem

Sunset Bay Mangrove Estuary

A sheltered mangrove estuary offering kayaking and boat tours through dense mangrove channels rich with juvenile fish, crabs, and seabirds — an excellent complement to reef-based marine tourism.

Coastal Municipality Mangrove Estuary

Travel Tips & Visitor Information

  • Bring or rent snorkeling gear — basic equipment is available at most coastal tourism spots.
  • Dive operators in Dapitan City offer PADI courses and guided reef dives for all experience levels.
  • Marine sanctuaries prohibit fishing, collection of coral or shells, and use of spear guns.
  • Best diving visibility is typically from November to May during the dry season.
  • Sea turtle encounters are possible year-round — keep a respectful distance and do not touch.
  • Support community-managed marine sanctuaries by paying the required visitor fees — funds go to reef protection.
