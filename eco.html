Eco-Tourism

Eco-Tourism

Immerse yourself in the lush forests, biodiverse wetlands, and pristine natural landscapes of Zamboanga del Norte.

Zamboanga del Norte's interior is a world of remarkable ecological diversity. Dense rainforests blanket the highland ranges of the Zamboanga Peninsula, while coastal mangrove ecosystems, freshwater lakes, and protected wildlife areas offer habitats for rare and endemic species found nowhere else on Earth.

Eco-tourism in the province is growing as communities and local government units work together to protect natural areas while offering meaningful nature experiences to visitors. From guided forest treks to mangrove kayaking and bird-watching tours, the province's eco-tourism offerings are as varied as its landscapes.

Eco-Tourism Destinations

Discover the natural wonders and protected areas of Zamboanga del Norte.

Protected Forest

Zamboanga Peninsula Biodiversity Corridor

One of the most biodiverse forest corridors in Mindanao, home to endemic birds, mammals, and plant species. Guided forest treks can be arranged through local eco-tourism cooperatives.

Interior Highlands Forest Reserve
Coastal Wetland

Mangrove Eco-Park, Dipolog

A community-managed mangrove park along the coast of Dipolog offering boardwalk trails, kayaking, and bird-watching. An excellent introduction to coastal ecosystem conservation.

Dipolog City Mangrove Park
Highland Forest

Linabo Peak Reforestation Area

The forested slopes surrounding Linabo Peak are part of an active reforestation zone offering scenic hikes through pine and dipterocarp forest, with panoramic views across the peninsula.

Dipolog City Eco-Hiking
Freshwater Lake

Lake Dumpao

A peaceful inland lake surrounded by lush vegetation and home to diverse bird species. Popular with bird-watchers and nature photographers seeking tranquility off the beaten path.

Interior Zamboanga del Norte Lake Ecosystem
Marine Ecology

Sigayan Bay Seagrass Beds

The extensive seagrass meadows of Sigayan Bay are a critical feeding ground for marine life, including sea turtles. Guided snorkeling tours highlight the underwater ecosystem.

Sibutad Marine Ecology
River Trail

Miputak River Eco-Trail

A community-led eco-trail following the Miputak River through secondary forest and traditional Subanen settlements, offering insight into both ecology and indigenous stewardship of natural resources.

Dipolog City River Eco-Trail

Travel Tips & Visitor Information

  • Always hire a registered local guide when entering forest reserves and protected areas.
  • Do not collect plants, animals, or soil samples from any protected eco-tourism site.
  • Wear long-sleeved clothing and apply insect repellent before entering forest areas.
  • Carry out all trash — 'leave no trace' principles are strictly observed in eco-parks.
  • Best bird-watching season is early morning (5–9 AM) from October through March.
  • Contact the local DENR office for permits to visit protected zones.
