Camping Tourism

Sleep under a canopy of stars in the forests, mountains, and beaches of Zamboanga del Norte.

For travelers who want to experience Zamboanga del Norte's natural beauty in its most immersive form, camping offers an unparalleled connection with the landscape. From beachside campsites where you fall asleep to the sound of waves to highland forest clearings where the stars blaze in unpolluted skies, camping in the province is a genuine back-to-nature experience.

Organized campsite infrastructure is limited but developing, with several beach resorts, eco-parks, and barangay-managed areas now offering designated camping spaces with basic facilities. For the self-sufficient camper, the forests, islands, and mountains of the province offer a wealth of wild camping opportunities with the right preparation and local knowledge.

Camping Sites & Overnight Destinations

Set up camp and spend a night beneath the stars of Zamboanga del Norte.

Beach Camping

Dakak Beach Campsite

Dakak Beach Resort offers a designated beachside camping area for visitors who want to experience the famous beach under the stars. Basic facilities are available, and the resort's amenities are accessible to campers.

Dapitan City Beach Campsite
Summit Camping

Linabo Peak Summit Camp

Adventurous hikers can camp at a designated area near the Linabo Peak summit for a sunrise experience above the clouds. Bring full camping gear as no facilities are available at the summit.

Dipolog City Summit Camp
Island Camping

Aliguay Island Overnight

Arrangements can be made with local boatmen to camp overnight on Aliguay Island — a remote island camping experience with pristine reef snorkeling in the morning and a star-filled sky at night.

Dapitan City Island Camp
Remote Island

Sibutad Island Camping

The white-sand shores of Sibutad's barrier islands offer idyllic camping spots for self-sufficient campers. Visitors must bring all supplies as no facilities exist on the smaller outer islands.

Sibutad Wilderness Beach Camp
Forest Camping

Forest Camp, Interior Eco-Parks

Community-managed eco-parks in the province's interior offer designated forest camping clearings with fire pits and basic shelter. An immersive experience for those who want genuine rainforest camping.

Interior Municipality Forest Camp
River Camping

Riverside Camping, Salug River

Shaded riverbank campsites along the Salug River offer a cool and scenic camping experience near freshwater swimming holes. Best visited in the dry season when river levels are low and water is clear.

Salug Riverside Camp

Travel Tips & Visitor Information

  • Always obtain permission from barangay officials or resort management before setting up camp.
  • Bring a mosquito net and insect repellent — biting insects are active at dusk and dawn.
  • Pack a waterproof tent and rain fly — tropical weather can change rapidly even in dry season.
  • Follow strict leave-no-trace principles: pack out all trash and avoid damaging vegetation.
  • Island camping requires boat transport — arrange with local boatmen the previous day and confirm return timing.
  • Inform local authorities or your accommodation of your camping location and planned duration.
