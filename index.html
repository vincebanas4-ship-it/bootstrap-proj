Welcome to Zamboanga del Norte

Discover the beauty, heritage, and spirit of the Land of the Subanen — a province of pristine coastlines, living indigenous culture, and a history that shaped the Filipino nation.

Pearl of the North  ·  Lupa ng mga Bayani  ·  Diyos Magtampol

Zamboanga del Norte is a province located in the Zamboanga Peninsula (Region IX) of Mindanao, Philippines. Its capital is Dipolog City, and it includes the historic City of Dapitan. The province is bordered by the Sulu Sea to the west and the Bohol Sea to the north, giving it a long and scenic coastline rich with marine biodiversity. With a population of over one million people, it is home to diverse communities — including the Subanen, Visayan, and Maranao peoples — making it a vibrant mosaic of cultures, traditions, and languages.

The province is renowned for its natural wonders: from the world-class Dakak Beach Resort in Dapitan to the pristine islands of Sibutad and the rolling highlands of the interior. Its coastline stretches across two major bodies of water, offering white-sand beaches, coral reefs, and breathtaking seascapes. The Zamboanga Peninsula's forests are home to rich biodiversity, making it an emerging eco-tourism destination. From the mountains to the sea, Zamboanga del Norte is a province of extraordinary natural abundance.

Beyond its natural gifts, Zamboanga del Norte holds a treasured place in Philippine history. The City of Dapitan is the exile home of Dr. José Rizal — the national hero — who lived, taught, and practiced medicine there from 1892 to 1896. Today, the Rizal Shrine stands as a monument to his legacy. The Subanen people, one of the Philippines' oldest indigenous groups, keep their traditions alive through their crafts, dances, and ceremonies.

Discover Zamboanga del Norte

Location of Zamboanga del Norte

Zamboanga del Norte is situated in the Zamboanga Peninsula (Region IX) of Mindanao, bordered by the Sulu Sea to the west and the Bohol Sea to the north — a coastal province of remarkable geographic beauty.

Official Seal of Zamboanga del Norte

The Official Seal reflects the identity of Zamboanga del Norte — its colors symbolizing the land, the sea, and the unity of its diverse peoples bound together in pride and purpose.

Artist of Zamboanga del Norte

Subanen weavers are celebrated for their intricate handwoven textiles and traditional patterns that carry deep cultural meaning passed through generations — living art that defines the soul of the province.

Tourist Spot

Dakak Beach Resort in Dapitan City — one of the Philippines' premier beach destinations, featuring powdery white sand, crystal-clear waters, and lush tropical surroundings that enchant every visitor.

People of Zamboanga del Norte

The people of Zamboanga del Norte are known for their warm hospitality and rich cultural diversity — a proud blend of Subanen, Visayan, and Maranao heritage united by shared love of their homeland.

Food in Zamboanga del Norte

The Knickerbocker is Dapitan's beloved signature dessert — a refreshing, colorful mix of fruits, jellies, and ice cream unique to this province and a must-try for every traveler who passes through.

Cities & Municipalities

Component Cities

Dipolog City Provincial Capital

The provincial capital and commercial hub of Zamboanga del Norte. Known as the Orchid City of the Philippines, Dipolog serves as the seat of provincial government, main air gateway, and primary center of trade, education, and health services for the entire province.

Dapitan City

One of the most historically significant cities in the Philippines, Dapitan is the exile home of national hero Dr. José Rizal (1892–1896). Home to the Rizal Shrine, Dakak Beach Resort, and Jose Rizal Memorial State University.
